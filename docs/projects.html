This page presents selected professional projects and MBA case studies that combine engineering, operations, finance, and digital transformation.

Professional Projects

Concrete Pipes Market Expansion – Truck-Mounted & Stationary Pumps (China–Mexico, 2019)

Context:
After massive steel imports from China and a booming market driven by competitors, Mexican concrete pumping customers needed more competitive prices on pipe systems for both truck-mounted and stationary pumps (lay-down and high-rise applications), creating an opportunity to find new partners to maintain market share.

My Role:
Commercial lead and project manager for identifying, analazing and developing a new international supplier.

What I did:

  • Research, identified and evaluated Chinese steel pipes manufacturers for concrete pumps.
  • Coordinated technical validation with service teams and key customers.
  • Structured pricing, margin, and inventory proposals for the Mexican market.
  • Prepared commercial materials, technical training and supported sales teams during launch.

Results / Impact:

  • Increase the company’s market share by approximately 25% in the concrete pipe segment.
  • Contributed to an estimated 15% increase in sales for this product line.
  • Strengthened the company’s position in both truck-mounted and stationary pump projects.
Lubricants Distributor Financial Analysis – Mobil Exxon vs Shell (Mexico, 2017)

Context:
Aftermarket management needed to choose the best long-term partner between two lubricant distributors (Mobil Exxon vs Shell) for parts and service in construction and mining equipment customers.

My Role:
Consultant analyst commercial and financial evaluation.

What I did:

  • Performed ratio and trend analysis (profitability, liquidity, leverage, efficiency).
  • Reviewed historical sales volumes, margins, and payment behaviour.
  • Presented an executive summary with risks, opportunities, and recommendations.

Results / Impact:

  • Provided a structured, data-driven resport comparison that clarified the financial and operational differences between the two alternatives.
  • Decision-making support by selecting the distributor with better credit behaviour, stronger growth potential, and profitability.
National Retrofit Campaign – Truck-Mounted Concrete Pumps (Mexico, 2016)

Context:
Putzmeister brand in its main product the truck-mounted concrete pumps (sold in 2014–2015) required a turret retrofit to prevent failures and ensure long-term reliability.

My Role:
Supervisor for the national retrofit campaign.

What I did:

  • Worked with the PM factory to define technical scope, and procedures.
  • Built a national schedule and risk plan for all affected units and clients.
  • Negotiated downtime windows with customers to minimize disruption.
  • Coordinated service teams, spare parts, and documentation.

Results / Impact:

  • Completed the campaign across the national fleet using project crashing techniques, resulting in a reduction of the overall timeline by about 10 days
  • Improved customer trust and confidence
  • Strengthened collaboration between service, parts, and sales teams.

MBA Academic Projects

Digital Transformation Strategy – Lumen5 (CMPT 641)

Focus:
Analysis and evaluation on how Lumen5 can move from a creator-oriented freemium tool to an enterprise-grade video platform.

My Role:
Co-author of recommendations and roadmap, lead for Ecosystem Growth recommendation.

Key Points:

  • Used Sociotechnical Systems (STS) and TOE frameworks to analyze misalignments between the product, organization, and market.
  • The recomendation of a re-positioning strategy which focuses on governance, ROI, security, and scalability for marketing teams (enterprise).
  • Designed a 24-month roadmap including partnerships, integrations, and data-driven value.

Impact / Learning:

  • Developed a stronger understanding of how to connect technical capabilities, business model, and organizational change.
400 kW Rooftop Solar Power Plant – Project Management Plan

Focus:
Created a full project management plan for a 400 kW rooftop solar power plant.

My Role:
Project planning and scheduling.

Key Elements:

  • Built a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and Risk Breakdown Structure (RBS).
  • Developed a detailed schedule in ProjectLibre with task dependencies, critical path, ES/EF/LS/LF, and slack.
  • Assigned resources (labor, equipment, materials) and estimated total duration and budget.
  • Performed basic project crashing analysis to reduce the duration by 5 business days.

Impact / Learning:

  • Practiced applying project management tools to a real-world renewable energy context.
  • Strengthened skills in risk management, scheduling, and cost/time trade-offs.
